The Morph House

22 december – 10 january · outskirts of east london · 4–5 people · funded accommodation · hosted by pranshul

What this is

the morph house is a winter experiment i'm running: a few weeks in a shared house where people can actually give time to the things they care about, without needing to perform or "build fast". this is a space to work on passion projects—those interests you've been wanting to pursue for a long time but didn't have the time.

for a while, a regular house on the edge of east london becomes a soft studio for ideas: people writing at the kitchen table, reading on the sofa, playing with code or paint, going for cold walks, and going down rabbit holes together.

there is no strict programme. just gentle structure, shared meals, and a group of people each tending to something that matters to them.

Some things you might do here

  • work on drawings, writing, research, music, philosophy, maths, or something in-between.
  • develop a small idea: a zine, a talk, a tiny game, a thought-through essay.
  • explore a topic properly: a question, a field, a weird niche you've been circling.
  • gently reconnect with your thinking / creative self after a messy year.

you do not need a perfectly defined "project". a direction, question, or hunch is enough.

People & culture

this is meant to be an interdisciplinary house. you're welcome whether you come from art, philosophy, maths, science, social sciences, movement, community work, design, writing, or none of those boxes.

the main thing is that you care about ideas or making things, and you're up for living gently with other humans while you do that.

this is not a tech "builder house", startup incubator, or productivity bootcamp. laptops are fine; pitch decks are not the point.

you don't need a portfolio, followers, or an art school background. curiosity, kindness, and respect for the house are the important bits.
